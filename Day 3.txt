1.Learn what semantic tags are and why they are important for SEO & accessibility. Compare semantic vs non-semantic tags.
      Semantic tags clearly describe the meaning of the content inside them.
      They help browsers, search engines, and screen readers understand the structure of the webpage.

    Tag	             Meaning
  <header>	Top section of a webpage
  <nav>	        Navigation links
  <main>	Main content area
  <section>	Section of related content
  <article>	Independent content (blog, news)
  <aside>	Sidebar content
  <footer>	Bottom section of a webpage

Why Semantic Tags are Important:
   SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
   Search engines understand the page structure easily.
ex:
   <header> → page heading
   <article> → important content
   <footer> → page information

Accessibility: 
   Screen readers used by visually impaired users can understand the page structure.
Ex:
   Screen reader can jump directly to navigation or main content.

    Semantic Tags	Non-Semantic Tags
      <header>	            <div>
      <footer>	            <span>
      <article>	             <b>
      <section>	             <i>

sematic ex:                   
  <header></header>                
  <nav></nav>
  <footer></footer>

non-sematic ex:
  <div class="header"></div>
  <div class="menu"></div>
  <div class="footer"></div>
